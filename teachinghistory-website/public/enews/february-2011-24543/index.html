Winter counts, or *waniyetu wowapi*, are Lakota pictorial histories or calendars, with one picture representing each year. According to the Smithsonian, *waniyetu* means a “year, which is measured from first snowfall to first snowfall…*Wowapi* means anything that is marked on a flat surface and can be read or counted, such as a book, a letter, or a drawing.” Search the winter count image database and learn about the Lakota culture through accompanying videos.
