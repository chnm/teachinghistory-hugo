  • NC.1. Course / Competency Goal: African American Studies

    The learner will assess the influence of geography on the economic, political, and social development of slavery in the United States.

    • 1.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the economic, political, and social reasons for focusing the slave trade on Africa.

    • 1.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the role of geography on the growth and development of slavery.

    • 1.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Assess the impact of the slave trade on Africa and the colonies.

    • 1.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Investigate the Middle Passage as one of the largest forced migrations in human history.

  • NC.2. Course / Competency Goal: African American Studies

    The learner will develop an understanding of the justifications and ramifications of slavery between 1619 and 1860.

    • 2.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the economic, social, religious, and legal justifications for the establishment and continuation of slavery.

    • 2.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Discuss and evaluate the various ways Africans in America resisted slavery.

    • 2.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the role of African Americans in the development of the United States as a new nation.

    • 2.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Assess the role slavery played in the development of nationalism and sectionalism.

    • 2.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Trace the development of the abolitionist movement and its impact on slavery and the nation.

  • NC.3. Course / Competency Goal: African American Studies

    The learner will demonstrate an understanding of African American life and cultural contributions through 1860.

    • 3.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Compare and contrast African American urban and rural communities in the North and the South.

    • 3.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Discuss and analyze the black family in antebellum America.

    • 3.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Trace the development of African American institutions such as religion, education, and benevolent organizations.

    • 3.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify the contributions of African Americans in science and the arts.

  • NC.4. Course / Competency Goal: African American Studies

    The learner will analyze the roles of African Americans during the Civil War and Reconstruction.

    • 4.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Evaluate President Lincoln’s changing position on African Americans.

    • 4.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify the roles of black soldiers, spies, and slaves to the war effort in both the North and the South.

    • 4.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the effects of Reconstruction on the legal, political, social, cultural, educational, and economic life of freedmen.

    • 4.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the successes and failures of Reconstruction.

  • NC.5. Course / Competency Goal: African American Studies

    The learner will examine the rise of Jim Crow and its effects on the life experiences of African Americans in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.

    • 5.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Assess the economic impact of Jim Crow laws on African Americans.

    • 5.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Investigate the legal ramifications of segregation laws and court decisions on American society.

    • 5.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Compare and contrast the political movements that developed in response to Jim Crow laws including, but not limited to, the Niagara Movement, the NAACP, the Urban League, and the Anti-Lynching Crusade.

    • 5.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Compare and contrast the African American political and legal personalities of the time period and their impact on American society.

    • 5.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Evaluate the economic, cultural, political, and social impact of African American migration within and from the South.

    • 5.06. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the impact of black regiments on the western campaigns, the Spanish American War, and World War I.

  • NC.6. Course / Competency Goal: African American Studies

    The learner will analyze the cultural contributions made by African Americans during the Harlem Renaissance.

    • 6.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Assess the literary contributions made by African Americans.

    • 6.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the contributions of African Americans to dance and music.

    • 6.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify the contributions of African Americans in the visual arts.

    • 6.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Evaluate the impact of the black media on American life.

    • 6.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the reactions of Americans to the Harlem Renaissance.

    • 7.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the impact of the Great Depression and the New Deal on the lives of African Americans.

    • 7.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Evaluate the continued quest for civil rights in America.

    • 7.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the effects of black ‘pop’ culture of the 1930’s and 1940’s.

    • 7.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

    • 7.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe and evaluate the contributions of African Americans during World War II.

    • 7.06. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Explain how World War II laid the groundwork for the modern Civil Rights Movement.

  • NC.8. Course / Competency Goal: African American Studies

    The learner will analyze the successes and failures of the Civil Rights Movement in the United States.

    • 8.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Explain how legal victories prior to 1954 gave impetus to the Civil Rights Movement.

    • 8.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the impact of Brown v. Board of Education of Topeka, Kansas and evaluate the resistance and reaction to it.

    • 8.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Define various methods used to obtain civil rights.

    • 8.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify various organizations and their role in the Civil Rights Movement.

    • 8.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Assess the extent to which the Civil Rights Movement transformed American politics and society.

    • 8.06. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Determine the impact of the Vietnam War on the Civil Rights Movement.

  • NC.9. Course / Competency Goal: African American Studies

    The learner will acquire an understanding of the issues confronting contemporary African Americans in the continuing struggle for equality.

    • 9.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify and analyze how the changing political environment has impacted civil rights.

    • 9.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe how African Americans have responded to political conservatism.

    • 9.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Compare and contrast the responses of African Americans to the economic, social, and political challenges of contemporary America.

    • 9.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify and evaluate major contemporary African American issues confronting society including, but not limited to, affirmative action, the educational achievement gap, the wealth gap, poverty, AIDS, and crime.

    • 9.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the impact of immigration and migration on the lives of African Americans in contemporary America.

    • 9.06. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify the major contributions of contemporary African Americans in business, education, the arts, politics, sports, science, technology, and society in general.

  • NC.1. Course / Competency Goal: American Government

    The learner will identify the basic foundations of the American political system and assess major changes that have occurred.

    • 1.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Recognize important European influences on the development of the American governmental system.

    • 1.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify fundamentals of the United States Constitution and changes made over time.

    • 1.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Distinguish between federal and state powers as established in the United States Constitution.

    • 1.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the intent of the Founding Fathers in the creation of our early documents.

    • 1.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the influences of the early documents on our present plan of government.

  • NC.2. Course / Competency Goal: American Government

    The learner will display knowledge of the basic functions and structures of the American political system at the local, state, and federal levels.

    • 2.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify the similarities and differences in the functions of government at each level.

    • 2.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Differentiate between enumerated, reserved, and concurrent powers.

    • 2.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Evaluate examples of separation of powers and the system of checks and balances.

    • 2.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze between local, state, and federal governmental enforcement powers on decisions and policies.

    • 2.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Assess legislative, executive, and judicial activities at the state and national levels.

  • NC.3. Course / Competency Goal: American Government

    The learner will examine the role and analyze the influence of political parties in the American political process.

    • 3.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

    • 3.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Evaluate the creation of third parties in American politics and their effect on political outcomes.

    • 3.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the reasons for non-political participation.

  • NC.4. Course / Competency Goal: American Government

    • 4.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Pose rationales for the formation of political interest groups.

    • 4.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Evaluate various lobbying techniques for their legal, ethical, and practical uses.

    • 4.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Differentiate between the pros and cons of political action groups system.

      Identify the legal rights of American citizens according to the Bill of Rights.

    • 5.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Distinguish between civil and criminal law.

    • 5.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Construct the steps in the legal process.

    • 5.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Examine and pose solutions to legal problems in the United States.

  • NC.6. Course / Competency Goal: American Government

    The learner will analyze the basic rights and responsibilities of citizenship.

    • 6.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Explain the rights guaranteed to citizens in both the United States and North Carolina Constitutions.

    • 6.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Differentiate between the legal and ethical responsibilities of citizenship.

    • 6.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Determine processes that influence civic issues and public actions.

    • 7.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the reasons for change in international relationships throughout history.

    • 7.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

  • NC.1. Course / Competency Goal: American Indian Studies

    The learner will examine American Indian life prior to the arrival of Columbus.

    • 1.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the Paleo-Indian, Archaic, Woodland, and Mississippian phases of American Indian prehistory.

    • 1.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Explain the technological changes which occurred during prehistory.

    • 1.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the lifestyles of American Indians in prehistory such as subsistence, settlement patterns, and social organization.

    • 1.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the adaptations of American Indians to local environments.

    • 1.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify how North Carolina fits in the larger prehistoric context, including regional studies of archaeological sites.

  • NC.2. Course / Competency Goal: American Indian Studies

    The learner will analyze the historical developments that characterize Native American life in the period prior to the Civil War.

    • 2.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Explain the effects of contact and conflict between American Indians and Europeans.

    • 2.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Assess the impact of exploration and colonization of the Americas by Spain, France, England, and other European powers.

    • 2.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Demonstrate an understanding of the impact on North Carolina tribes of the events of this period, such as the Tuscarora War and the disenfranchisement of the states’ American Indians in 1835.

    • 2.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Assess the impact of the major events of the period including, but not limited to, the Iroquois Confederacy and the Great Law of Peace; the removal of American Indians from the East; and the participation of American Indians in the Civil War.

    • 2.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe colonial and federal treaty relationships with American Indians.

    • 2.06. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify the major American Indian leaders, male and female, of this period, and explain their significance in the development of U. S. history.

  • NC.3. Course / Competency Goal: American Indian Studies

    The learner will analyze the historical developments that characterize Native American life in the period from the Civil War to the present.

    • 3.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the conflicts between American Indians and Europeans after the Civil War, with special focus on westward expansion and its effects on Indian land.

    • 3.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify and explain the major events of the period including, but not limited to, the Apache Wars, the Flight of the Nez Perce, the Long Walk of the Navajo, Custer’s Defeat, Wounded Knee (1890), the Trail of Broken Treaties, the Occupation of Alcatraz, and the Seizure of Wounded Knee (1973).

    • 3.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the federal policies, which impacted American Indians, such as forced assimilation, allotment, federal boarding schools, compensation termination, relocation, and tribal self-determination.

    • 3.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the involvement of North Carolina tribes in the events of this period, including but not limited to the Lowrie War, the establishment of all-Indian schools in the state’s tribal communities, and the establishment of the North Carolina Commission of Indian Affairs.

    • 3.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify the major American Indian leaders, male and female, of this period, and explain their significance to U. S. history.

  • NC.4. Course / Competency Goal: American Indian Studies

    The learner will investigate the diversity of American Indian tribal cultures.

    • 4.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify and describe significant characteristics of major cultural areas of North American Indians.

    • 4.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Compare and contrast American Indian language families, such as Iroquoian, Algonkian, and Siouan.

    • 4.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Demonstrate a general knowledge of American Indian art, music and spirituality, including the modern day powwow.

    • 4.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Explain the connection between American Indian kinship and family structures.

    • 4.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Compare the worldviews of American Indians and mainstream society, such as concept of time, relationship to the natural universe, and circularity versus linearity.

    • 4.06. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Describe the traditional and contemporary cultural characteristics of North Carolina tribes.

  • NC.5. Course / Competency Goal: American Indian Studies

    The learner will analyze contemporary issues that face American Indians.

    • 5.01. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the major socio-cultural issues of concern to American Indians, including cultural identity, stereotypes, and relations with non-Indians.

    • 5.02. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Analyze the major political issues of concern to American Indians including tribal sovereignty; land, water, hunting and fishing rights; and relationships with federal and state governments.

    • 5.03. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Explore the major issues of spirituality which concern American Indians including religious freedom, the Native American Church, protection of sacred sites, and repatriation.

    • 5.04. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Identify major health issues of concern to American Indians including, but not limited to, diabetes, hypertension, asthma, and substance abuse and analyze their impact on American Indians.

    • 5.05. Competency Goal / Objective:

      Discuss the contemporary issues that affect North Carolina Indian tribes.
