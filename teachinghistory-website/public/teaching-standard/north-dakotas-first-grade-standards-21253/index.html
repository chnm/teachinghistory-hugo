  • ND.1. Content Standard: Skills and Resources

    Students apply Social Studies skills and resources.

    • 1.1.1. Benchmark: Map Skills

      Identify basic components of maps and globes (i.e., cardinal directions, map key, symbols)

    • 1.1.2. Benchmark: Map Skills

      Identify current event sources (e.g., news publications, Internet, radio, TV)

  • ND.2. Content Standard: Important Historical Events

    Students understand important historical events.

    • 1.2.1. Benchmark: Family History

      Compare past and present family life (e.g., housing, transportation, technology) over time

    • 1.2.2. Benchmark: Concepts of time

      Define chronological terms for broad categories of historical time (i.e., long ago, yesterday, today, tomorrow)

    • 1.2.3. Benchmark: People and Events

      Identify the people and events honored in United States holidays (e.g., Thanksgiving, Independence Day, Veteran’s Day, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, President’s Day, Memorial Day, Columbus Day, Labor Day)

    • 1.2.4. Benchmark: People and Events

      Describe the interaction (e.g., survival, First Thanksgiving) of Native Americans with the Pilgrims

  • ND.3. Content Standard: Economic Concepts

    Students understand economic concepts and the characteristics of various economic systems.

    • 1.3.1. Benchmark: Personal Finances

      Differentiate between wants and needs

    • 1.3.2. Benchmark: Personal Finances

      Describe ways (e.g., money, bartering) wants and needs are met

  • ND.4. Content Standard: Government and Citizenship

    Students understand the development, functions, and form of various political systems and the role of the citizen in government and society.

    • 1.4.1. Benchmark: Citizenship

      Apply good citizenship (e.g., respect, cooperation, honesty, responsibility ) in the school environment

    • 1.4.2. Benchmark: Citizenship

      Explain the purpose for rules in home, school, and community

  • ND.5. Content Standard: Concepts of Geography

    Students understand and apply concepts of geography.

    • 1.5.1. Benchmark: Physical Geography

      Identify Earth’s geographical landforms (e.g., islands, mountains, plains, hills, bodies of water)

  • ND.6. Content Standard: Human Development and Behavior

    Students understand the importance of culture, individual identity, and group identity.

    • 1.6.1. Benchmark: Culture

      Identify the similarities and differences (e.g., family members, clothes, food, style of homes, jobs, celebrations) among families around the world
