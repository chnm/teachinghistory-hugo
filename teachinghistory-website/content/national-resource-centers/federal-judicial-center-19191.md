The Federal Judicial Center is the education and research agency for the federal courts. [History of the Federal Judiciary](http://www.fjc.gov/history/home.nsf) includes background information about the court system, judicial legislation, federal courthouses, and oral histories. A [biographical directory](http://www.fjc.gov/public/home.nsf/hisj) of judges since 1789 includes all federal courts.
[Teaching Judicial History: Federal Trials and Great Debates in United States History](http://www.fjc.gov/history/home.nsf/page/teaching.html) provides in-depth instructional units with narrative and supporting documentation from [The Sedition Act Trials](http://www.fjc.gov/history/sedition.nsf/autoframe?openform&header=/history/sedition.nsf/page/header_home&nav=/history/sedition.nsf/page/nav_home&content=/history/sedition.nsf/page/home) to [Bush v. Orleans Parish School Board and the Desegregation of New Orleans Schools](http://www.fjc.gov/history/bushvorleans.nsf/autoframe?openform&header=/history/bushvorleans.nsf/page/header_home&nav=/history/bushvorleans.nsf/page/nav_home&content=/history/bushvorleans.nsf/page/home).
