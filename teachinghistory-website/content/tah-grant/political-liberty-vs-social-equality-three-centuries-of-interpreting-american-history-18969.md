By building teachers' knowledge of traditional American history and helping them translate that knowledge into higher student achievement, this project seeks to meet the professional development needs of a district in which the percentage of students with limited English proficiency has increased over 4,000 percent in the past 15 years. Five integrated instructional components will support teachers in developing historical thinking skills and improving instructional skills. The project focuses on the relationship between political liberty and social equity in American history, exploring how to frame historical questions in Year 1, how to analyze evidence in Year 2, and how to present historical arguments in Year 3. Topics range from Lewis and Clark's visit to Idaho to Japanese internment in the state.
