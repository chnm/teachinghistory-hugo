The "Frontier" partnership is aimed at providing professional development in American history content and instruction, building on progress made under a previous TAH grant. While the first grant effectively provided teachers with a survey of history up to the Civil War, this project will extend the subject matter to the present day and create and core group of 30 master teachers. Sustainability will be encouraged by creation of an Innovation Configuration document to guide classrooms in the future. Topics in Year 1 include: the Louisiana Purchase, Lewis and Clark, Texas Annexation, California gold rush, and Nez Pierce Treaty. In Year 2, teachers will study: the transcontinental railroad, Chinese-Japanese immigrants, Idaho's statehood, the 1905 crisis, and World War I. Year 3 focuses on globalization, the New Deal, World War II, Civil Rights movement, and the environmental movement.
