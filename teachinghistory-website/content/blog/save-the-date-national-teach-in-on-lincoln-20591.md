The [History Channel](http://www.history.com) and the [Abraham Lincoln Bicentennial Commission](http://www.abrahamlincoln200.org/) are offering a National Teach-In on the life and legacy of Abraham Lincoln on Thursday, February 12, 2009, at 1:30EST. (The History Channel also publishes a minisite including [videos and essays](http://www.history.com/topics/abraham-lincoln) on Lincoln.)

The Teach-In features two Lincoln Scholars: [Matthew Pinsker](http://web.archive.org/web/20130730114830/http://dickinson.edu/news-and-events/news/2008-09/Matthew-Pinsker,-Featured-Scholar-at-National-Teach-In/) and [Harold Holzer](http://www.haroldholzer.com/). They will share their expertise and answer student questions from throughout the country. Content recommended for middle through high school, with an emphasis on eighth grade.

Questions? Please email lincoln@aetn.com.

Please consult [A New Look at Abraham Lincoln](/nhec-blog/20291) and [Lincoln Bicentennial](/nhec-blog/20737) for previous articles on classroom resources for the bicentennial.
