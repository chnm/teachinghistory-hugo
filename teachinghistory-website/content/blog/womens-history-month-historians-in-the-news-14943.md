Some historians are women who sometimes make history themselves. Drew Gilpin Faust, historian, author, and president of Harvard University, discusses her career, the history profession, and her new book, [*This Republic of Suffering: Death and the American Civil War*](http://www.amazon.com/This-Republic-Suffering-Death-American/dp/037540404X/ref=pd_bbs_sr_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1202493908&sr=8-1) in [The Washington Post](http://www.washingtonpost.com) of February 7, 2008.

Laura Thatcher Ulrich, president-elect of the [American Historical Association](http://www.historians.org), reads from her recent book, [*Well-Behaved Women Seldom Make History*](http://www.amazon.com/Well-Behaved-Women-Seldom-Make-History/dp/1400041597/ref=pd_bbs_2?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1202493975&sr=1-2) on the [Making History Podcast Blog](http://web.archive.org/web/20220807215938/http://www.makinghistorypodcast.com/2008/01/31/episode-4-part-1-laurel-thatcher-ulrich/), a venue fostering discussion about the writing process. In  [Part Two of the podcast](http://web.archive.org/web/20220807214812/http://www.makinghistorypodcast.com/2008/02/10/episode-4-part-2-laurel-thatcher-ulrich/), Ulrich offers advice to aspiring historians and discusses the challenges of research.
