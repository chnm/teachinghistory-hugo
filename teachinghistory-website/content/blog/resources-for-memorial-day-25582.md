Observed on the last Monday of May, our modern Memorial Day has its roots in two wars. In the late 19th century, "Decoration Day" honored the Civil War dead. After World War I, the holiday expanded to honor all citizens who died in service to the U.S. (though it would not become an official national holiday until 1971).

[![](/files/image-md-sp.jpg)](/spotlight/memorial-day)

Ready to learn more about Memorial Day? Visit our Memorial Day [spotlight page](/spotlight/memorial-day) for teaching strategies, quizzes, website reviews, videos, and more on the history of the holiday. Listen to a [presentation](/history-content/history-in-multimedia/25565) on dog tags, discover high school teacher James Percoco’s [techniques](/best-practices/using-primary-sources/24079) for engaging students with monuments and memorials, or test your knowledge of the [54th Massachusetts](/history-content/quiz/24192), the Civil War regiment made famous in the film *Glory*. Other organizations also offer rich Memorial Day resources. Try visiting:

* The U.S. Department of Veteran Affairs for the [history](http://www.va.gov/opa/speceven/memday/) of Memorial Day and associated traditions.
* The Library of Congress for [primary sources](http://memory.loc.gov/ammem/today/may30.html), including 19th- and early 20th-century [newspaper articles](http://www.loc.gov/rr/news/topics/memorialday.html) on the holiday.
* The Smithsonian National Museum of American History for online exhibits on [military history](http://americanhistory.si.edu/militaryhistory/) and the [diary](http://americanhistory.si.edu/documentsgallery/exhibitions/nursing_1.html) of a Civil War nurse.
* The National Park Service for information on historic battlefields, [cemeteries](http://www.nps.gov/history/nr/travel/national_cemeteries/), [monuments and memorials](http://www.nps.gov/nacc/index.htm), and other sites related to military history. Check out [lesson plans](http://www.cr.nps.gov/nr/twhp/topic.htm#military) on teaching with historic places, too.
* The White House for [presidential proclamations](http://search.whitehouse.gov/search?utf8=%E2%9C%93&query=%22memorial+day%22+-%22peace+officers%22&m=&embedded=&affiliate=wh&filter=moderate&commit=Search) from past Memorial Days.
* EDSITEment for [lesson plans](http://edsitement.neh.gov/subject/history-social-studies) on U.S. military history (click "War and Foreign Policy" under "Themes"), including a [lesson](http://edsitement.neh.gov/lesson-plan/massachusetts-54th-regiment-honoring-heroes) on the Massachusetts 54th Regiment.
* The Gilder Lehrman Institute of American History for a special issue of *History Now*, "[Perspectives on America’s Wars](http://www.gilderlehrman.org/history-now/2012-03/perspectives-america%E2%80%99s-wars)," and [essays](http://www.gilderlehrman.org/history-by-era/world-war-i/essays/from-these-honored-dead-memorial-day-and-veterans-day-american-his) and [primary sources](http://www.gilderlehrman.org/history-by-era/new-deal/resources/george-washington-would-have-supported-new-deal-1934) related to Memorial Day.
* PBS for [video clips](http://www.pbs.org/topics/history/wars-conflicts/) related to Memorial Day and military history, including on spouses and parents of fallen soldiers.
