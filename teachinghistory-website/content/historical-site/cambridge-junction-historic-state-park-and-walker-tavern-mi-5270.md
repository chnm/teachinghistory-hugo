Cambridge Junction Historic State Park is the site of Walker Tavern, a major stopping place for stagecoaches traveling between Detroit and Chicago in the early 19th century. The site tells the story of taverns and stagecoach travel for early Michigan residents.

The park offers exhibits and occasional recreational and educational events.
