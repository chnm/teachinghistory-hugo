Chimney Point of Lake Champlain in Vermont is notable for being one of the earliest and most intensely settled sites in the Champlain Valley. Chimney Rock has had human habitation for 7,500 years, and today the State Historic Site is focused on chronicling the history of human settlement at the location, from American Indians through the French colonials up to early American settlement.

The historic site offers exhibits, special events, and guided tours. The website offers visitor information, a history of the site, and a calendar of events.
