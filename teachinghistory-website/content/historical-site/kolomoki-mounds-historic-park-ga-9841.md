The 1,294-acre Kolomoki Mounds Historic Park contains a great temple mound, four ceremonial mounds, and two burial mounds built by the Swift Creek and Weeden Island Native Americans between the years 250 and 950.
The site offers exhibits, a film, and seven picnic areas. Picnic areas can be reserved.
