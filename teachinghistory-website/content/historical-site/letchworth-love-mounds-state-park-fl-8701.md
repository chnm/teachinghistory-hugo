Visitors to this archaeological site will see Florida's tallest Native American ceremonial mound—46 feet—built between 1100 and 1800 years ago. The people who built the mound are believed to have been members of the Weedon Island Culture, a group of Native Americans who lived in North Florida between 200 and 800 A.D.

The park offers tours.
