On April 6, 1865, known as the Black Thursday of the Confederacy, General Robert E. Lee's Army lost 7,700 soldiers in the Battle of Sailor's Creek. This defeat was the penultimate act of the Civil War, and Gen. Lee surrendered at Appomattox Court House only 72 hours later.

The location features the Overton-Hillsman House, which was used as a field hospital during the battle, and is open to visitors from June through August. The battlefield is open to visitors year-round, and is maintained in its 1865 condition. The site offers visitors basic historic information, visitor information, a listing of nearby attractions, and an events calendar.
