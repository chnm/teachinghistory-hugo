 [Picturing America](picturingamerica.neh.gov), a recent initiative from the National Endowment for the Humanities, contains a wealth of resources for using art in the classroom. The site contains links to four lesson plans that teach students how to analyze art, for example, teaching [the basics of composition](http://edsitement.neh.gov/curriculum-unit/composition-painting-everything-its-right-place). The site contains over [20 pieces of art](http://picturingamerica.neh.gov/index.php?sec=gallery) from various periods in U.S. history. A [short essay](http://picturingamerica.neh.gov/educators.php?subPage=edu_guide) with background information and analysis accompanies each piece of art. There are resource guides for using art, including [a guide](http://picturingamerica.neh.gov/educators.php?subPage=edu_headstart) designed specifically for younger students.
