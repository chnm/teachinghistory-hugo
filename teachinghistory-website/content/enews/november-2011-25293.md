  |  | The Election of 1932: Clifford Berryman Cartoon Political cartoons are great
  teaching tools because they provide a glimpse into what people were thinking about
  in the past. Today we may think that the 1932 election of Franklin Roosevelt was
  a foregone conclusion due to the Great Depression. But at the time, many commentators
  thought President Hoover would be reelected. Watch as Senate Historian Donald Ritchie
  decodes the symbolism of a political cartoon that captures the momentum shift in
  the 1932 presidential campaign. [View here.](http://teachinghistory.org/best-practices/examples-of-historical-thinking/25169)
  |  |  |  | Life, Liberty, and the Pursuit of Happiness Looking for a rich collection
  of lesson plans that span the U.S. history curriculum for grades 4-12? South-central
  Connecticut''s Area Cooperative Educational Services'' (ACES) TAH program *Life,
  Liberty, and the Pursuit of Happiness* created an online repository of over 80 lesson
  plans developed by teachers. Additionally, you can visit their blog, *Keeping it
  Real*, which compiles lessons and teaching strategies for incorporating primary
  sources into the classroom. [Learn more.](http://teachinghistory.org/tah-grants/project-spotlight/25159)
