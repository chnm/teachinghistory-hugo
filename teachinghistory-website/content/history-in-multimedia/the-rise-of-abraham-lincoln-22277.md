From the Maine Humanities Council [website](http://mainehumanities.org/podcast/archives/946):
"Before he was the leader of a nation torn apart by a Civil War, Abraham Lincoln was a young man growing up during tumultuous times in Illinois. In the first presentation of the [Maine Humanities Council's] [Lincoln Bicentennial Symposium](http://mainehumanities.org/special-programs/lincoln.html), historian Bruce Chadwick explained Lincoln's rise to power from his first unsuccessful race for the state legislature to his election as President."
