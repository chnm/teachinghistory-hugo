This exhibition commemorates the 50th anniversary of the pivotal 1954 *Brown* v. *Board of Education of Topeka, Kansas* case. It offers 116 images, including book covers, letters, political cartoons, and photographs. [Exhibition Overview](http://www.loc.gov/exhibits/brown/brown-overview.html) is a 300-word introduction to the exhibit and its significance.
The website is divided into three sections: [A Century of Racial Segregation](http://www.loc.gov/exhibits/brown/brown-segregation.html), [*Brown* v. *Board of Education*](http://www.loc.gov/exhibits/brown/brown-brown.html), and [The Aftermath](http://www.loc.gov/exhibits/brown/brown-aftermath.html), all of which consist of links to documents, detailed paragraphs on selected documents, and events related to that section. "Discover" buttons are dispersed throughout these exhibit sections; and, when clicked, reveal more information and answer a particular question, such as "What is 'separate but equal?'"
The [Exhibition Checklist](http://www.loc.gov/exhibits/brown/brown-checklist.html) includes links to all images used on the site. The site is an ideal resource for students interested in the historical developments that led to the *Brown* v. *Board* decision.
