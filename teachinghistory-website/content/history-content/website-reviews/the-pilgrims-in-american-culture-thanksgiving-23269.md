Documenting the colonial experience of settlers in Plimoth, MA, this site contains several brief articles on Puritanism, traditional thanking for harvest, the Wampanoag, the formation of Thanksgiving as a national holiday, Thanksgiving menu regionalism and commonality, and primary source quotations. A major focus of the site is New England Thanksgiving. A somewhat longer article focuses on the [menu of the original Thanksgiving](http://www.plimoth.org/learn/thanksgiving-history/partakers-our-plenty). Explore the online activity about [What really happened at the First Thanksgiving?](https://www.plimoth.org/learn/MRL/interact/thanksgiving-interactive-you-are-historian) that invites you to be the historian and explore multiple perspectives.

A virtual tour of the Plimoth Plantation, which requires a free browser plug-in, allows visitors to observe the grounds as well as historical re-enactors and interpreters. Although the site has relatively few primary sources, the exhibit is useful as an introduction to New England studies.
