The [Office of History of the National Institutes of Health](http://web.archive.org/web/20200507131519/https://history.nih.gov/index.html) is the repository for the history of the federal agency itself and for narratives of the history of the medical profession and medical research.

[*A Short History of the National Institutes of Health*](http://web.archive.org/web/20200811194913/https://history.nih.gov/exhibits/history/) starts with the roots of the agency in 1887 as a one-room laboratory and moves into the present time, highlighting biomedical discoveries and government policy.

[Over a dozen online exhibits](http://web.archive.org/web/20200507124828/https://history.nih.gov/museum/virtual.html) cover topics including [Medical Instruments from 1945–1965](http://web.archive.org/web/20200507151124/https://history.nih.gov/museum/virtual_instruments.html), [Deciphering the Genetic Code](http://web.archive.org/web/20200507145509/https://history.nih.gov/exhibits/nirenberg/index.htm), and a [history of medical illustration](http://web.archive.org/web/20200507150927/https://history.nih.gov/exhibits/Bartner/index.html).

For teachers, an [Education](http://history.nih.gov/museum/education.html) section offers lesson plans on the history of science.
