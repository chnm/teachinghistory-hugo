The [United States Patent and Trademark Office](http://www.uspto.gov/) site does not feature an explicit history section, but the [Kids' Pages](http://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/ac/ahrpa/opa/kids/index.html) include a number of activities and information associated with historical topics and research methods. The [Time Machine Section](http://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/ac/ahrpa/opa/kids/kidevents.html) includes a [Calendar](http://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/ac/ahrpa/opa/kids/calendar/index.html) of Famous and Not-So-Famous Patents and Trademarks, [Historic Press Releases](http://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/ac/ahrpa/opa/kids/kidevents_press.html), access to [patents](http://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/ac/ahrpa/opa/kids/usptodatabases.htm) dating back to 1790 and the story of [The Great Patent Fire of 1836](http://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/ac/ahrpa/opa/kids/special/1836fire.htm).

Other features include an overview of [Women Inventors](http://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/ac/ahrpa/opa/kids/ponder/ponder8.htm), a brief history of [trademarks](http://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/ac/ahrpa/opa/kids/ponder/ponder2.htm), and [How to Be a Patent Detective](http://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/ac/ahrpa/opa/kids/kidsearch.html).
