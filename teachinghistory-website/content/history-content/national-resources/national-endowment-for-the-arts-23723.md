According to their site, the National Endowment for the Arts strives toward "supporting excellence in the arts, both new and established; bringing the arts to all Americans; and providing leadership in arts education."
The most exciting content on the site for educators is definitely the [audio & video](https://www.arts.gov/videos) section. Listen to [Ray Bradbury](https://www.arts.gov/video/nea-big-read-meet-ray-bradbury) discuss *Farenheit 451*; interviews and tributes to [opera greats](https://www.arts.gov/video/nea-opera-honors-interview-richard-gaddes) Carlisle Floyd, Richard Gaddes, James Levine, and Leontyne Price; videos of [jazz masters](https://www.arts.gov/video/2008-nea-jazz-masters-panel-part-1) Joe Wilder, Candido Camero, Quincy Jones, Gunther Schuller, and Tom McIntosh; and Richard Bausch on [how to write](https://www.arts.gov/video/richard-bausch-letter-young-writer).
