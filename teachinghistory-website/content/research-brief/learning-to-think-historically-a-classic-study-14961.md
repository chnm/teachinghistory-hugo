* Recognize that many of your students will come to your classroom with beliefs about history that may prevent them from learning what you want to teach them.
*Work to uncover students' preexisting beliefs.** Start the school year with a "what is history" exercise. For example, bring in two different textbook accounts of the same event or contrast a new textbook's account with an older one.
*Students will need practice with this type of exercise to overcome their initial confusion and gradually become more adept at dealing with multiple accounts.** Help students understand the connections between analyzing historical accounts and figuring out which of two modern newspaper accounts to believe.
*Present students with short sources (such as those listed in the Sample Application) and help them see the relationship between what is said and who says it.*
