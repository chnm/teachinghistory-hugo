The [National Museum of the American Indian](http://nmai.si.edu/home/) in Washington, DC offers a variety of resources about American Indian history including workshops for teachers.

For resources you can use with students, see our response to a teacher who asked about classroom resources for [teaching a Native American history course](/teaching-materials/ask-a-master-teacher/22901).

We also recommend contacting local tribes and organizations directly to see what resources they recommend that you use to learn about their history. Below are some organizations you might consider:
> We also recommend contacting local tribes and organizations directly to see what resources they recommend that you use to learn about their history.

In Connecticut, the Mashantucket Pequot Museum & Research Center provides a wealth of resources. They offer [professional development](http://www.pequotmuseum.org/Home/EducationalResources/TeacherServices.htm) to teachers as well. The center also offers workshops on how to evaluate books and other materials about Native Americans and have several educational programs for students based on the Connecticut Curriculum framework. The center designs [workshops based on teacher interest as well](http://www.pequotmuseum.org/uploaded_images/6923A989-2396-4F83-9D99-B7C894065C2E/journeythroughtime.pdf). The Mashantucket Pequot Museum & Research Center provides a [recommended reading list](http://www.pequotmuseum.org/Home/EducationalResources/SuggestedReading.htm) and a [research library](http://www.pequotmuseum.org/Home/EducationalResources/LibraryResources.htm).

If you will be in Minnesota for the summer you may want to check out the [American Indian Policy Center](http://www.airpi.org/aboutus.html ) in St. Paul and [The Minnesota Indian Affairs Council](http://www.indianaffairs.state.mn.us/index.html). These organizations offer resources, and can most likely direct you to additional educational resources.

You can search the NHEC site for relevant [local museums](/history-content), [websites](/history-content), and [professional development opportunities](/digital-classroom). If you have not done so already, remember to also check the course offerings at your local colleges.
